Privacy Policy — Stride

Last updated: July 2026

Stride ("the app") is a simple step, distance and calorie tracker built by Olajide Samuel. This page explains what data the app uses and, just as importantly, what it does not do with it.

What data the app accesses

  • Step / activity data — Stride reads step count data from your phone's built-in motion sensor (via the Android Activity Recognition permission) so it can show you how many steps you've taken.

What the app does NOT do

  • It does not collect your name, email, phone number, or any personal identifiers.
  • It does not use GPS or location data.
  • It does not require an account, login, or sign-up.
  • It does not send any data to a server. There is no backend — the app has no server at all.
  • It does not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties, advertisers, or analytics services.

Where your data is stored

All step counts, goals, streaks, and history are stored locally on your device only, using standard Android local storage. This data never leaves your phone. If you uninstall the app, this data is permanently deleted along with it.

Permissions the app requests

  • Activity Recognition / Body Sensors — required so the app can read your phone's step counter. This is the only permission the app requests.

Children's privacy

Stride does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children, since it does not collect personal data from any user at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future update adds a new feature that uses data differently — this page will be updated, and the "Last updated" date above will reflect that.
